Memory Palace

Independent Project

This is a VR exploration of your photo library. Using a photo's metadata, images are projected into 3D space according to date and location. Images are re-sampled every few seconds to make the experience dynamic and engaging. Made with Unity, scripts written in C#.

Raytracer

Independent Project

I built a raytracer from scratch, written in C++. Images can be rendered with orthographic or perspective viewing. The system also supports several geometric objects, including spheres, triangles, and planes, and also supports several types of lights, including ambient, directional, and point lights. The program also supports several BRDFs and materials.

VR Horticulture Simulator

Project Lead

This VR project places users in the University of Illinois Conservatory, where they can navigate the environment and learn more about the plants housed there. To achieve this, we took a 360 video of the conservatory, and inserted informational cards that the user can interact with. Our team hoped to promote relaxation and an interest in nature.

October 2017 - December 2017

Automated Hitching System

Research Team Lead

In September of 2016, the University of Illinois chapter of the Society of Women Engineers (SWE) partnered with John Deere to create an automated hitching system. Over the course of the project I was able to fulfill several roles. I served as a research team lead for one of our mechanical systems, which allowed me to manage a multidisciplinary team, help guide the project, and identify our skillsets so that we could best develop our designs. After completion of the mechanical system, I lead the displays team. We developed a Pygame system which implemented triangulation methods to provide visuals to the tractor operator. We also built a Raspberry Pi prototype to test our systems.

In November of 2017 we presented this project at the SWE national conference along with teams from other universities. Our team was awarded first place!

Interests

Stories are how we make sense of the world. They allow us to connect, to learn, and to empathize. By listening to the stories of others and sharing our own, we can work to form a more connected and inclusive world.

I'm thrilled to be a software engineer and to see all of the amazing developments that are occurring in the storytelilng space. More specifically, I'm interested in Computer Graphics, VR/AR, and realtime technologies. I believe advancements in these technologies will enable us to tell more impactful stories and to engage with wider audiences than ever before. I’m incredibly excited about experiencing and contributing to the future of storytelling.
